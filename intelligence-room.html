The real problem

You are making six-figure decisions on a gut feel.

Either you do not have a clear strategy to anchor in, or you have the data and no way to make sense of it. Both end the same way: budget spent on instinct instead of evidence.

01

Your strategy is a stale PDF

You paid for a deck. It looked sharp on day one. Then the market moved and it became a file nobody opens. Strategy happens once a quarter. Buying does not.

02

Your data is scattered in six places

LinkedIn says one thing. Google says another. HubSpot, website visitors, intent. Nobody pulls it into one picture, so nobody can answer where to scale and where to cut.

03

Budget is bleeding into off-ICP accounts

Spend follows whoever clicks, not whoever fits. A real slice of every dollar lands on accounts that were never going to buy. You feel the waste. You just cannot name it.

04

You cannot tell what is actually working

CAC is creeping. Some channel is carrying the load and some channel is dead weight, but the reporting will not tell you which. So you fund both and hope.

The shift

There is one number almost nobody in B2B is tracking.

In the accounts we track, the gap between an account with stacked buying signals and one with none is not small. It is the difference between a coin flip and a long shot.

The mechanism is Signal Aggregation. Most companies look at signals one at a time, in separate tools. We stack them: ad exposure, engagement, website visits, intent, profile views, follows. When the signals pile up on an ICP-fit account, that account is telling you it is ready.

Straight answers

The questions skeptical leaders ask.

What is the difference between Foundations and the Impact Report? +

Foundations clarifies direction when you are setting or resetting strategy: positioning, targeting, messaging, ecosystem. The Impact Report makes sense of data you already have to fix efficiency and waste. Founders setting direction usually start with Foundations. Leaders with live spend and a CAC problem usually start with the Impact Report. Plenty of teams do both.

Is this a retainer or a one-time project? +

One-time. Both sprints are fixed scope and fixed price. You can stack channel strategies at $1,000 each, and many teams come back for a fresh diagnostic each quarter, but there is no lock-in to get started.

What do I actually walk away with? +

A custom marketing intelligence room plus every document from your sprint, exported and structured for both your team and your AI tools. You own all of it.

What do you need from me? +

For Foundations, context on your business and your market. For the Impact Report, read access to the channels and data you run: LinkedIn and Google ad accounts, your CRM, website visitor data, any intent tool. We can start with whatever you have.

How is this different from an intent tool like 6sense or Demandbase? +

Those tools give you a feed of signals. We aggregate signals across sources, score them against your real ICP, and hand you the strategy and the activation plays. Tools tell you something is happening. The room tells you what to do about it.

Will the numbers survive leadership scrutiny? +

That is the point. Every number is built same-period, same-definition, same-attribution, with the math shown and the caveats stated plainly. It is built to survive a skeptical CFO, not to look pretty in a slide.
