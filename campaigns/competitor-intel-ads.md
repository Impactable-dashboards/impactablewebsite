## STAGE 1: Unaware to Aware (Cold / TOF)
**Job:** earn attention, reframe the problem. No case studies, no demo CTA. Formats: Thought Leader Ads, video, single image.

### Ad 1A: Thought Leader Ad (Justin Rowe)
**Hook:** Your agency reports clicks. Your CEO asks about pipeline.
**Body:**
Most LinkedIn reporting is a monthly dump of impressions and CPM. None of it answers the only question your CEO has: which accounts moved.

We rebuilt the whole thing around that question. Compose the signals into a qualified account list before launch. Tie ad exposure to named accounts in the CRM. Report the revenue you can trace back.

On our own spend, that system returned 6.7x.
**CTA:** Follow for the B2B Demand Intelligence playbook.

### Ad 1B: Single Image
**Headline:** Your ICP filter returns 4 million people.
**Subhead:** Ours returns the accounts actually moving.
**Body:** LinkedIn native targeting is a pool of millions, run and hoped on. We compose 8 signals into a qualified list before a dollar is spent. Waste is prevented at the list stage, not explained after.
**CTA:** Learn more

- The accounts actually moving toward a decision. Find yours.
- Stop guessing your ICP. Let the engagement data tell you.

## Notes for the creative team
- Lead every cold concept with something ownable (the system, signal composition, named-account proof). Never open on "wasted spend" or "data-driven."
- Every claim maps to the proof inventory in the brief. If a number is not on that list, do not use it.
- One orange accent max in creative. Core palette navy/blue, black, white, neutrals, canopy/moss green (per 06-visual-identity.md).
- Gut-check before shipping: confident, specific, honest? Leads with something ownable? Real number or named mechanism, not an adjective? Zero em-dashes, zero hype words? Speaks to the exec's real fear (defending spend to the board)?
