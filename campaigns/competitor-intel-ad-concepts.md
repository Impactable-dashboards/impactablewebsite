# 20 Ad Concepts: Competitor Intel (BOF / Warm) - Round 2

**Why round 1 missed:** strong-ish copy, but the design defaulted to the same abstract node-map on
every frame, so nothing reads as a valuable asset to grab. Fixes below, then 20 locked
text + design pairings.

**Also correct from round 1:** it is TWO documents (Competitor Intel Report + Audience Targeting
Worksheet), not three. Do not use "287 accounts." Per brand, say "the accounts actually moving."

## Design principles (read first)
1. **Make the free thing look expensive.** Show the actual deliverable: report covers, a filled-in
   worksheet, a dossier. A tangible artifact beats an abstract graphic every time.
2. **One idea per frame, huge.** The hook must read at thumb size. If it needs a second look, cut it.
3. **Show the grab.** Tilt the asset, fan two covers, stamp it FREE. Make it feel handed to them.
4. **Kill the sameness.** Do not put a node map on every ad. Vary the device: document, matrix,
   funnel, highlighter, redaction, stopwatch, map.
5. **One orange, for the CTA only.** Core navy/blue, black, white, neutrals, canopy/moss accents.
6. **Honesty sells the value.** No fake price tags. Use "built by hand," "the same diagnostic we
   run before a client's first dollar," "60 to 90 minutes of our analysts." All true, all valuable.

**CTA options (keep short):** "Send your 3 competitors" / "Grab the free reports" / "Get the intel" / "Claim your 2 reports"

## Bucket A: make "free" feel valuable (no-brainer grab)

**01.** On-image: **"The competitor teardown we run before a client's first dollar. Yours, free."**
CTA: Send your 3 competitors.
Design: a polished report cover tilted in 3D, titled "Competitor Intel + Targeting," with a canopy-green "FREE" wax seal stamped on the corner. It looks like a real, valuable deliverable being handed over.

**02.** On-image: **"Two documents. Built by hand. $0."**  Sub: "Competitor intel and a targeting worksheet, in 48 hours."
Design: two report covers fanned side by side, each labeled (Competitor Intel / Audience Targeting). A bold "$0" as the hero element. Clean, high-contrast, premium.

**03.** On-image: **"Most agencies charge for this. We do it free to earn the work."**
Design: an invoice-style sheet with the total line reading "$0.00" highlighted in canopy. Disarming and honest.

**04.** On-image: **"60 to 90 minutes of our analysts. On your competitors. Free."**
Design: a stopwatch / timecode motif beside a report being built. Signals real human effort, not an auto-PDF.

**05.** On-image: **"A free asset your CMO will actually read."**
Design: the report on a desk, gaps highlighted in canopy, a coffee ring. Editorial, human, premium.

---

## Bucket B: process beats output

**06.** On-image: **"A report is the output. The system is the point."**
Design: brutal contrast. Left, tiny and grey, a flat "report.pdf." Right, dominant, a living engine/dashboard (not a thin node tree). The gap should be obvious in half a second.

**07.** On-image: **"Anyone can pull a competitor PDF. We hand you the engine behind it."**
Design: a flat PDF dissolving into a working dashboard with signals and named accounts.

**08.** On-image: **"This is not a competitor report. It is a targeting plan you can run Monday."**
Design: a worksheet with fields and checkboxes already filled in. Action-ready, not theory.

---

## Bucket C: competitor intel / espionage

**09.** On-image: **"We will reverse-engineer your top 3 competitors' LinkedIn playbook."**  Sub: "Then show you the lanes they leave wide open."
Design: competitor names as redacted black bars, a magnifier revealing one. Intrigue.

**10.** On-image: **"Every ad, offer, and angle your competitors run. In one document."**
Design: a grid of competitor "dossier" cards, surveillance-board style, clean not cluttered.

**11.** On-image: **"Your competitors' positioning, decoded. The open lanes, mapped."**
Design: a landscape map with rival flags clustered in one corner and a glowing open territory.

**12.** On-image: **"What are your competitors saying that you should stop saying?"**
Design: a wall of identical saturated phrases ("data-driven," "precision targeting," "reduce wasted spend") greyed and struck through, with one open lane glowing canopy. (Shown as their claims to dial back, never ours.)

## Bucket D: actionable data (show the goods)

**13.** On-image: **"The 4 audiences your competitors are not targeting."**  Sub: "Yours, mapped, in 48 hours."
Design: the four targeting-source cards (first-party warm, LinkedIn filters, third-party signals, in-market search) as a crisp preview.

**14.** On-image: **"From 4,000,000 to the accounts actually moving."**
Design: a clean narrowing funnel (native pool, then filters plus 8 signals, then the short list). Replaces the vague node tree with a clear, legible funnel.

**15.** On-image: **"Your defensible UVP, in one sentence. We will write it for you."**
Design: a document with a blank line resolving into a highlighted UVP statement.

**16.** On-image: **"The warm audience you already own and probably ignore."**
Design: a spotlight on "site visitors, newsletter, engagers" with a value tag. An insight they feel.

## Bucket E: proof-led (warm reminder)

**17.** On-image: **"6.7x on our own spend. See what we would build for yours."**  Sub: "Free."
Design: a huge "6.7x," attribution line in mono ("on our own LinkedIn spend"), offer block beneath with the orange CTA.

**18.** On-image: **"You know our content. Now get our intelligence, free."**
Design: a nod to the newsletter (28.8K) and following (40K+) flowing into the report cover.

**19.** On-image: **"The same diagnostic behind a 6.7x return. Run on your market."**
Design: the report cover with a small proof ribbon. Pairs method and result.

---

## Bucket F: provocative / pattern interrupt

**20.** On-image: **"You are one document away from knowing exactly who to target."**
Design: a single bold document with a clear grab gesture or arrow. Simplicity as the hook.

## How to test
- Test the **buckets** first (A value, B process, C espionage, D actionable, E proof, F provocative). One winner per bucket into a head-to-head.
- Hold offer and audience constant. Read on cost per report request, then report-to-call rate.
- For the winning bucket, test headline and CTA wording.

## Guardrails (every frame)
Confident, specific, honest. No em-dashes. No leading with saturated claims (they appear only as
competitors' phrases to dial back). Sanctioned proof only: 6.7x, 5x, $50M+/200+ campaigns,
Lacework 6x, HeyReach 20x, Clutch 4.5/5, LinkedIn Marketing Partner, 28.8K newsletter, 40K+ followers.
Two documents, not three. "The accounts actually moving," never an invented count.
