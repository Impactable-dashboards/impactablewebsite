# Creative Brief: Competitor Intel Ads (BOF / Warm)

**For:** Claude design (brand assets already loaded)
**Deliverable:** LinkedIn ad creative, copy + image/design, multiple variants to test
**Date:** June 2026
**Anchored to:** brand-core docs (positioning, competitors, audience, messaging-offer, brand-voice, visual-identity)

---

## 1. The one-paragraph brief

Build scroll-stopping LinkedIn ad creative that drives **warm, bottom-of-funnel** B2B
marketing and revenue leaders to claim our free **Competitor Intel Report + Targeting
Worksheet**. These people already know Impactable. The job is not to introduce us. The job
is to convert familiarity into a hand-raise by proving that the free report is a real slice
of the system we run for paying clients. Sell the **process and the output**, not just the
output. Anyone can email a competitor PDF. We hand you the method behind it: signal
composition, 200+ campaigns of pattern, and a team that returned 6.7x on its own spend.

---

## 2. Objective and placement

- **Funnel stage:** Bottom of funnel. Warm retargeting and engaged audiences only.
- **Audiences:** site visitors (90d), ad engagers / video viewers 50%+, newsletter, event attendees, page followers, CRM. Filtered to buyer-ICP. Agencies and peers excluded.
- **Primary action:** claim the free Competitor Intel Report (reply with competitors / land on `/competitor-intel-report.html`).
- **Success metric:** cost per report request, then report-to-call rate. Not impressions.

## 3. Audience (who is reading this)

**VP / Exec Economic Buyer.** CMO, VP Marketing, VP Growth, CRO. 200 to 1,000 employees.
B2B SaaS/tech, IT services, financial services. They own the budget and the board narrative.
Their real fear: defending LinkedIn spend they cannot trace to revenue. They do not want more
tactics. They want proof and a partner who already knows the field.

## 4. The offer (what we are pushing)

**Competitor Intel Report + Targeting Worksheet.** Three documents, built for you, free,
48-hour turnaround:
1. **Competitor Intel** - what your top rivals run on LinkedIn: angles, offers, positioning.
2. **Open Lanes Report** - the gaps they leave open that you can own.
3. **Audience Targeting Worksheet** - the signal-composed account-list logic, signal sources, persona splits.

Trigger: "Reply with your top 3 competitors." Four-field form on the landing page.

---

## 5. The big idea: process AND output

This is the spine of every concept. The free report is not the prize. It is **evidence of the
machine**. Make the viewer feel the difference between:

- **The output everyone else gives:** a static competitor PDF. Generic. Forgettable.
- **The output plus the process we give:** competitor intel mapped against 200+ campaigns of
  signal data, the open lanes scored, and a targeting worksheet built the exact way we prep a
  paying client before we spend a dollar.

The creative should make a warm buyer think: "If this is what they give away for free, the
paid version is the system I have been missing." Lead the eye from the artifact to the engine
behind it.

**UVP to convey (in plain terms):** Impactable is the B2B Demand Intelligence System. We tell
you who your buyers actually are, engineer the account list before a dollar is spent, and tie
exposure to named accounts and revenue. The report is a window into that.

---

## 6. Voice and guardrails (hard rules)

**Voice:** Confident. Specific. Honest. Founder-to-peer. Plain words. Proof over adjectives.

**Never use (saturated, banned):** "wasted ad spend," "data-driven," "precision targeting,"
"LinkedIn Ads specialists/experts," "pipeline, not leads," "revenue over vanity metrics,"
"full-funnel demand gen," "$XM managed" as the lead claim, "we are not a generic agency."

**Mechanical:** No em-dashes anywhere. Use periods, commas, parentheses, or " - ". No hype
words (revolutionary, game-changing, unlock, supercharge, seamless, elevate). No "it is not
just X, it is Y." No "leverage" or "utilize."

**Proof bank (cite ONLY these, never invent a number):**
- 6.7x return on our own LinkedIn spend
- 5x close rate on multi-signal accounts
- $50M+ managed across 200+ campaigns (support, never the headline anchor)
- Lacework 6x ROI, HeyReach 20x ROAS
- Clutch 4.5/5, LinkedIn Marketing Partner (Agency + Tech)
- 8-signal composition stack
- Justin Rowe: 40K+ followers, 28.8K newsletter

**Honesty:** say "on our own spend" and "LinkedIn-sourced." Never imply "we drove $X" for a
prospect we have not worked with.

## 7. Visual identity direction

- **Palette:** navy/blue core (ties to the LinkedIn association), black, white, neutrals.
  Accents: canopy + moss green. One orange maximum, reserved for the CTA / highest-contrast
  element. Keep total accents to 5-6.
- **Signature motif: vine + node.** Organic growth meeting a network/data structure. This maps
  perfectly to signal composition: nodes are signals and accounts, vines are the connections.
  Use it to show millions narrowing to the few accounts actually moving, or to map a competitor
  landscape with open lanes glowing.
- **Type:** bold condensed display for the hook (Archivo Black on-site), clean sans for body
  (Inter), mono for data labels and stats (JetBrains Mono). (Fraunces is under review per the
  brand notes, so avoid relying on it.)
- **Tone of imagery:** engineered, intelligent, calm confidence. Data made legible, not busy.
  Avoid stocky "business handshake" clichés and generic dashboards.
- **Contrast:** mobile-first. The hook must read at thumb size in the LinkedIn feed.

## 9. Three creative concepts

### Concept A: Output vs Engine (lead concept)
- **Format:** Single Image (1080x1080 and 1200x628).
- **Visual:** split composition. Left, small and grey: a plain "Competitor Report.pdf" icon,
  flat and generic (what everyone else gives). Right, dominant: the vine + node network in
  brand blue/green, alive and connected (our process). An orange CTA chip anchors the right.
- **Headline:** "A report is the output. The process is the point."
- **Support:** "Free competitor intel, the open lanes, and a signal-composed targeting worksheet. Built for you in 48 hours."
- **Why it works:** dramatizes the exact gap we own. Warm buyers instantly get that we give more than a file.

### Concept B: Competitor Landscape Decoded
- **Format:** Single Image or 3-card Document/Carousel.
- **Visual:** a competitor landscape rendered as a node map. Rival positions clustered and
  saturated, with two or three **open lanes glowing** in canopy green. Mono data labels.
- **Headline:** "We will decode your top 3 competitors' LinkedIn playbook. Free."
- **Carousel beats (if document):** 1) The field is crowded. 2) Here is where everyone is
  saturated. 3) Here are the open lanes you can own. 4) Plus the targeting worksheet to win
  them. 5) Reply with your 3 competitors.
- **Why it works:** makes the abstract offer concrete and visual, and the "open lanes" become a
  thing they want to see for their own name.

### Concept C: Proof to Offer (warm reminder)
- **Format:** Single Image (1080x1080).
- **Visual:** one hero stat, "6.7x," set in bold display over a quiet node texture, attribution
  line in mono ("on our own LinkedIn spend"). Offer block beneath with the orange CTA.
- **Headline:** "You know our work. Here is a free piece of it for your market."
- **Support:** "Competitor intel, open lanes, and a targeting worksheet. The same method we run for clients."
- **Why it works:** warm audiences respond to specific proof plus a low-friction ask. Honest
  attribution builds trust the competitors will not match.

---

## 10. Specs and deliverables

- **Sizes:** 1080x1080 (feed square, primary), 1200x628 (landscape), 1080x1920 optional for
  story/spotlight. Carousel cards 1080x1080.
- **Per concept:** at least 2 headline variants on the same layout for A/B testing.
- **Safe zones:** keep the hook and CTA clear of the lower-right "Sponsored" and reaction UI.
- **Accompanying post copy (intro text):** 1 to 2 lines, hook first, ends with the CTA. Example:
  "You have seen our work. Tell us your top 3 competitors and we will build you the same intel
  report we run for clients. Three documents, 48 hours, free."

## 11. Test plan

- Test **angle** first: Concept A (process vs output) vs Concept B (competitor decode) vs
  Concept C (proof to offer).
- Within the winner, test **headline** and **CTA** wording.
- Hold the offer and audience constant. Read on cost per report request, then report-to-call rate.

## 12. Gut-check before anything ships

1. Confident, specific, honest?
2. Leads with something ownable (process, signal composition, competitor intel, named proof), not a saturated claim?
3. A real number or named mechanism, not an adjective?
4. Zero em-dashes, zero hype words, no AI cadence?
5. Speaks to the exec's real fear (defending spend, knowing the field)?
6. Does it sell the process and the output, not just the output?

If yes to all six, it is on-brand and on-brief.
