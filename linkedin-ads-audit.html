The reality

Most LinkedIn accounts burn budget in the same six places.

✕

Off-ICP spend

Impressions pouring into seniorities, industries, and company sizes that never convert, unnoticed for weeks.

✕

Cold-first launches

Leading with the most expensive audience while warm pools sit unused.

✕

The wrong funnel

Expecting search-style bottom-funnel leads from a channel built to create and warm demand.

✕

No reachability map

No view of which target accounts LinkedIn cannot reach, so budget keeps chasing accounts that will never see the ad.

✕

No signal tracking

Clicks counted, accounts ignored. The companies quietly engaging never reach sales while intent is warm.

✕

Vanity reporting

Impression and CTR dashboards that cannot answer the only question that matters: which accounts are moving toward a decision?

What it covers

A 35-point inspection, across seven areas.

01

Account structure

Campaign architecture, objective fit, naming, and budget splits.

02

Targeting and waste

Every segment graded by efficiency, off-ICP spend flagged for suppression.

03

Reachability

Which target accounts and persona campaigns LinkedIn is reaching, and which have low or no impressions and belong on another channel.

04

Bidding and pacing

Bid strategy, frequency, and warm-first sequencing.

05

Creative

Format fit, message-to-persona match, and fatigue.

06

Tracking and attribution

Insight Tag, CAPI, and whether ad exposure is tied to real pipeline or just clicks.

07

Funnel coverage

Whether create, capture, and activate are all present, or whether the account is a one-stage guess.
